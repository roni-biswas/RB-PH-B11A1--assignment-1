Dr. Muhammad Yunus, born in 1940 in Bangladesh, is a Nobel Laureate, economist, and social entrepreneur known as the pioneer of microfinance. He founded Grameen Bank in 1983, revolutionizing poverty alleviation by providing small, collateral-free loans to the poor, particularly women, enabling them to start businesses and achieve financial independence. His innovative approach to social business has empowered millions worldwide.
In 2006, he and Grameen Bank jointly received the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to create economic and social development from below. Beyond microfinance, Yunus has promoted various social enterprises addressing healthcare, education, and environmental sustainability. His visionary ideas continue to inspire global movements for financial inclusion and social business.
